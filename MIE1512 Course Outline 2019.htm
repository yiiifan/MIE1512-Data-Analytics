Outline

 

This course is a research seminar that focuses on recent developments in the area of Data Analytics. Science, businesses, society and government are been revolutionized by data-driven methods. The increased access to large quantities of digital information has provided new opportunities for innovation. A new area of Data Analytics, known as Big Data, is made possible thanks to novel affordable techniques for processing huge amounts of data. This seminar provides an overview of data analytics concepts, approaches, and techniques, including distributed computations on massive datasets and frameworks for enabling large-scale parallel data processing on clusters of commodity servers. Emphasis is given to algorithmic techniques for analyzing Web Data. The course evaluation is based on course presentations, a lab quiz, and a project. The project goal is to prepare publishable research in the area of data analytics. (See https://www.mie.utoronto.ca/mie/grad/courses?semester_id=2&course_year=2017#area_7)

Pre-requisites: An undergraduate level course in Databases, such as MIE253 Data Modelling, or equivalent (in particular, hands-on knowledge of SQL).

Course material (lecture slides and lab materials) are available on Quercus. The course slides posted on portal contain the references to the course textbooks and to the assigned reading materials covered each week.
